Module M10 Day 13–14

Handoff & Portfolio

Ship the work, then tell its story.

Learning Objectives

  • Prepare a Figma file for handoff using Dev Mode annotations
  • Document spacing, color, and typography specs developers can copy directly
  • Flag interactive states (hover, focus, error) explicitly for engineering
  • Structure a portfolio case study around problem, process, and outcome
  • Edit a 2-week project down to its most defensible decisions

Core Concepts

Handoff is a conversation, not a file drop

Dev Mode reduces guesswork, but it doesn't replace a walkthrough. The best handoffs flag intent and edge cases a spec sheet can't capture on its own.

A case study isn't a portfolio of screenshots

Hiring managers and clients want to see how you think, not just what shipped. Structure the narrative around decisions, not deliverables.

  • Problem — what was broken, for whom, and why it mattered
  • Process — research, iterations, and what you ruled out and why
  • Outcome — what changed, ideally with a measurable result
  • Reflection — what you'd do differently with more time

3. Case Study Structure — Problem → Process → Outcome → Reflection

The four parts:

  • Problem — what was broken, for whom, and why it mattered
  • Process — research, iterations, what you ruled out and why
  • Outcome — what changed, ideally with a measurable result
  • Reflection — what you'd do differently with more time
ProblemProcessOutcomeReflection

Example

A case study led with "23% cart abandonment" — the data point that motivated the project — instead of opening with the final polished screen. Reviewers said it "read like a business case," not a gallery.

👉 Output: A narrative that shows how you think, not just what shipped

The Handoff Loop

Handoff isn't the final step in isolation — engineering questions during build often reveal gaps to fix in the next project's Define stage, and a case study draft often exposes decisions you never actually justified.

Simple Real-World Flow

  1. 1Annotate your Figma file in Dev Mode for one full flow (Dev Mode)
  2. 2Flag every interactive state explicitly (Documenting States)
  3. 3Draft a 4-sentence outline: Problem / Process / Outcome / Reflection (Case Study Structure)
  4. 4Cut anything that doesn't hold up to a follow-up question (Editing Down)
  5. 5Ship it — capstone demo day

Key Takeaways

  • Dev Mode reduces guesswork but doesn't replace a walkthrough
  • Document edge cases a spec sheet can't show
  • A case study should show reasoning, not just the final screen
  • Depth beats volume — two strong projects outperform ten shallow ones
