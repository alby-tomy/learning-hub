Module M05 Day 6–7

Wireframing

Move fast and break paper, not pixels.

Learning Objectives

  • Explain why low fidelity speeds up early-stage feedback
  • Run a Crazy 8s session solo or with a group
  • Know which fidelity level matches which stage of a project
  • Translate a wireframe into a mid-fi Figma layout
  • Critique a wireframe for content priority before visual polish

Core Concepts

Fidelity is a dial, not a destination

Low fidelity protects bad ideas from premature attachment — it's faster to throw away a box-and-line sketch than a polished mockup. Raise fidelity only once the structure is validated.

The three stops

Each stop in the fidelity ladder answers a different question.

  • Lo-fi — does the structure and flow make sense?
  • Mid-fi — does the layout and content hierarchy work at real scale?
  • Hi-fi — does the visual design and interaction feel finished?

Concept Deep Dive

Wireframing is about fidelity as a dial, not a destination — match the level of polish to the question you're actually trying to answer.

1. Lo-fi — Validate Structure and Flow

What happens here:

  • Paper sketches or boxes-and-lines
  • No color, no real copy, no temptation to polish

Key rule:

  • Paper is faster to throw away than a polished mockup — that's the point

3. Mid-fi — Validate Layout at Real Scale

What happens here:

  • Real content lengths (not "Lorem Ipsum")
  • Real component states: empty, error, loading
  • Still grayscale — no final colors or imagery yet

Key rule:

  • If it only works with perfect placeholder text, it doesn't actually work

The Fidelity Loop

You don't move lo-fi to mid-fi to hi-fi once — a mid-fi layout failure can send you back to lo-fi structure, and real content discovered in mid-fi can break an idea that looked great in lo-fi.

Simple Real-World Flow

  1. 1Run Crazy 8s on a screen (Lo-fi divergence)
  2. 2Pick the strongest 1–2 sketches
  3. 3Rebuild with real content and component states (Mid-fi)
  4. 4Apply final visual design (Hi-fi)
  5. 5Move to Prototyping

Key Takeaways

  • Low fidelity protects ideas from premature attachment
  • Crazy 8s trades perfectionism for volume, on purpose
  • Real content breaks layouts that placeholder text hides
  • Never skip straight to hi-fi — it creates false confidence early
